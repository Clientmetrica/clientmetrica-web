# Content & Copy Guidelines — Clientmetrica Web

> Source of truth: `contexto-corporativo-clientmetrica.md`
> All copy must be consistent with the corporate context document.

## Brand voice

**Tone:** Professional and innovative — formal but approachable.
**Register:** Always in Spanish for LATAM/Spain audience. English for global outbound pages.

## Core messaging principles

### Lead with business outcomes, not technology

Always frame value in terms of ROI, efficiency, and measurable results.

- ✅ "Reduce operational costs without hiring more staff"
- ✅ "Scale your operations without scaling your headcount"
- ❌ "We use large language models and automation pipelines"

### Speak to the ICP

Target audience: medium-to-large companies in Chile (primary) and globally (secondary). They are digitally mature and evaluating technology as a strategic lever — not beginners. Avoid over-explaining basic concepts. Assume business and tech literacy.

### Agnostic positioning

Clientmetrica is brand and technology agnostic. Never favor a specific vendor or tool in copy unless the client has already defined their stack.

### CORFO backing as trust signal

Mention CORFO recognition where relevant (About page, credibility sections). Framing: "Respaldados por CORFO" / "CORFO-backed innovation"

## Product naming

Always use exact casing:

- **MATY** — all caps
- **Juanito** — title case
- **Roboto** — title case

Never refer to them generically as "the bot" or "the tool" — they are AI Agents.

## What to avoid

- Jargon without context ("LLM", "RAG", "embeddings") — translate to business impact
- Unsubstantiated promises ("the best", "revolutionary", "world-class")
- Generic filler ("In today's fast-paced world...")
- Passive voice — prefer active, direct sentences
- Mixing languages within a single page (pick one per locale)

## Bilingual copy rules

- Spanish is always written first — it is the primary language
- English translations must be natural, not literal — adapt idioms
- Headlines in Spanish tend to be longer; English versions can be more concise
- CTAs should be action verbs: "Conoce MATY" / "Meet MATY"

## Page-level tone guidelines

| Page                                  | Tone                                |
| ------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| Homepage                              | Bold, confident, outcome-focused    |
| Product pages (MATY, Juanito, Roboto) | Specific, use-case driven, credible |
| About (Nosotros)                      | Human, strategic, trust-building    |
| Contact                               | Clear, low-friction, direct         |

## Company facts (always accurate)

- Industry: SaaS / Technology
- Market: Chile (primary), global (secondary)
- Stage: Growth (50–500 clients)
- Recognition: CORFO-funded innovation projects
- Team structure: CEO, CIDi, COO
- Tech stack: Microsoft 365, Azure, HubSpot, Claude.ai
